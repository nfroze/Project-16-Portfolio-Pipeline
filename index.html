About

From Incident Response to Infrastructure

18 months as a Police Constable in the Metropolitan Police taught me skills that don't appear on technical assessments: translating complex requirements for non-expert audiences, making decisions under pressure with incomplete information, and working within compliance frameworks where documentation actually matters.

I carried that into DevSecOps through intensive training at Cyber Agoge, led by a Global CISO with experience at KPMG, BAE Systems, and UK Government. Then 8 months of building — using Claude and ChatGPT to accelerate learning, but implementing and testing everything hands-on across 20 production-grade projects.

The combination is unusual: an LLM in Criminal Justice from Northumbria plus AWS, Terraform, and Kubernetes certifications. Security automation plus the instinct to document everything like it might end up in court. Technical depth plus the ability to explain why a deployment is blocked without making enemies.

DevSecOps Pipeline

02

AI Governance Framework

Compliance by default — unapproved workloads blocked before production.

Challenge
Teams deploy workloads without guardrails. Missing documentation, compliance violations, security gaps — all discovered after the fact during audits.
Solution
Policy-as-code at two levels. Sentinel policies validate infrastructure before Terraform applies. OPA Gatekeeper admission controllers enforce requirements at Kubernetes deployment. Nothing deploys without passing governance checks.

Governance enforcement shifted from audit findings to deployment gates.
